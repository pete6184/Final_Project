Purpose for Analysis

In the United States, lung cancer is the second most common cancer in both men and women. According to the American Cancer Society, in 2021, it is estimated that over 235,000 new cases will be diagnosed, and 131,000 people will die from lung cancer. While these numbers are disheartening, we know there are options for people diagnosed with lung cancer to be able to beat the disease, or at the very least prolong their life. In cases where chemotherapy is not an option, or is not working, another option is to have the cancerous and nearby lymph nodes removed via surgery.

Our goal for this project was to try and predict the survival rates for patients having thoracic surgery. The benchmark for the data was to see how many patients survive one year after having the surgery.


Problem to Be Solved

We are trying to determine whether thoracic surgery will extend the life of patients. The qualification in this study is whether patients survived one year after the surgery.


Machine Learning Models

We created multiple machine learning models to be able to predict whether the pre-existing conditions would lead to a 1 year post-surgery survival rate.


Data Set Information:

The data was collected retrospectively at Wroclaw Thoracic Surgery Centre for patients who underwent major lung resections for primary lung cancer in the years 2007-2011. The Centre is associated with the Department of Thoracic Surgery of the Medical University of Wroclaw and Lower-Silesian Centre for Pulmonary Diseases, Poland, while the research database constitutes a part of the National Lung Cancer Registry, administered by the Institute of Tuberculosis and Pulmonary Diseases in Warsaw, Poland.
